The Yunnan Snub-nosed Monkey is unique amongst the primates, living at 4,000m in the Himalayas of north-western Yunnan in rare high-altitude conifer forest. It is in grave danger of extinction: only fifteen hundred remain in isolated patches of forest between the Yangtze and Mekong rivers. Baima Xueshan Nature Reserve has been established to protect the monkey and its habitat, but conservation of the monkey has proven difficult. Find out more...


Yunnan ’99 is an initiative from Cambridge University Expeditions Society. Undergraduates from Cambridge and from Chinese Universities worked together this summer in Baima Xueshan Nature Reserve to work towards conservation based upon sound scientific research. Find out more...


People live and work in the same environment as the monkey. It is important to consider their needs and aspirations in any conservation management plan. We combined research of the monkeys' behaviour and habitat use with a Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA) in a village in the Nature Reserve near the monkey's home range. Find out more...
